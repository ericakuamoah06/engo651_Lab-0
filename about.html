Who is Eric Akuamoah?

Profile

Eric Akuamoah is a geospatial professional with a strong focus on research and data-driven solutions in geospatial technology. He has experience in applying GIS and spatial analysis to large-scale projects, aiming to contribute innovative approaches to sustainable development. Currently, he is eager to leverage his expertise in a dynamic research environment at the World Bank Group, committed to advancing global development initiatives through cutting-edge geospatial research. Professionally, Eric has a notable background. He served as an M&E Coordinator at Afarinick Company Limited in Ghana, employing GIS for agricultural sustainability projects. His tenure as a GIS Data Officer at Ghana Cocoa Board saw him survey and map cocoa farms using advanced technology.
At Losamills Consult Limited and Lands Commission in Ghana, he excelled as a Geomatics Engineer, mapping buildings, assets, and lands. His internship at Ghana Water Company Limited involved mapping assets and managing geodatabases.His research experience includes assessing the accuracy of UAVs and GPS on ground control points and exploring applications of inertial sensors in mobile phone step counting. He has also been involved in innovative projects like technology for carbon capture at craft breweries, integrating rainfall time series and GIS data for flood prediction in Bangladesh, and mapping seagrass meadows with satellite imagery. Eric's skill set is comprehensive, including certifications in first aid, mine health, and safety management, along with proficiency in software like ArcGIS, QGIS, and programming languages like Python and SQL. He demonstrates strong competencies in critical thinking, project management, and problem-solving.
